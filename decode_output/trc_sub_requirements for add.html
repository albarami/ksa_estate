Service Centers Requirements (Fuel Stations)
Service Centers Requirements

  • For the plots overlooking the streets with a width of 30 meters or 36 meters, it is prohibited to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots to become completely commercial. It is allowed to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots so that they become residential only.

  • The requirements of fuel stations and service centers must be applied by the sub-municipal before starting the project design or implementation.
  • Setback of tank openings from the surrounding and adjacent streets is done according to the Saudi Building Code.
  • Setback of kiosks or ATMs from the developed streets or public transport paths is done according to the approved building code.
  • Compliance with the health, technical, and safety conditions that must be met in collective housing for individuals, according to Ministerial Circular No. (4100549892) dated 6/12/1441 AH.

Requirements For Adding Kiosks and ATMs Within Existing Buildings and Vacant Lands

First/ Requirements for Adding Kiosks and ATMs Within Existing/New Commercial Projects


  • The length of the plot on the commercial street must not be less than 60 m in the event of a request for a kiosk or a mobile ATM, and in the event of requesting to set up a kiosk and an ATM together, the length of the plot on the commercial street must not be less than 100 m.
  • Building setback on the side of the commercial street must not be less than 20 m.
  • The ATM or the kiosk must not affect the parking spaces required for the building and the movement of cars within the project.
  • The entry and exit of the kiosk or the ATM should be within the main entrance and exit of the project, and in the event that the length of the land on the commercial street is 100 m or more, it is allowed to have an additional entrance and exit.
  • The setback of the kiosk or the ATM on the main street must be not less than 6 m, or one-fifth of the width of the sub-street, with a minimum setback of 3 m.
  • In the event that the building is located on two or more commercial streets, the entry must be through the entrance to the project, and if another entrance condition applies, it must be located (35 m) away from the intersection.
  • Kiosks and mobile ATMs are dealt with within gas stations according to the new gas stations regulation issued by Ministerial Circular No. (4100130682) dated 11/4/1441 AH.
  • The area of a kiosk with a toilet must not exceed 24 m2.
  • The height of the kiosk must not exceed more than one floor.
  • A toilet must be provided within the kiosk building, provided that it has a separate entrance from the outside.
  • Submitting a proposed plan for the site to be licensed to the General Administration of Building Permits for study.

Second/ Requirements for Adding Kiosks and ATMs Within the Vacant Lands:


  • Setback of the kiosk or the ATM on the main street must not be less than 6 m, one-fifth of the width of the sub-street with a minimum of 3 m, or according to the building code approved in the region.
  • The length of the plot on the commercial street must not be less than 20 m, with an area of not less than 600 m2.
  • A request waiting area must be provided to fit least two cars inside the site.
  • In the event that the site is on two or more commercial streets, the entrance and exit of the ATM or the kiosk must be at a distance of (35 m) from the intersection.
  • The area of a kiosk with a toilet must not exceed 24 m 2.
  • The height of the kiosk must not exceed more than one floor.
  • A toilet must be provided within the kiosk building, provided that it has a separate entrance from the outside.
  • An undertaking from the property owner or the lessee of the ATM or kiosk must be attached in the event of adding buildings, which provides compliance with the requirements of establishing an ATM or kiosk within the commercial buildings, and they must be removed in the event of breach.

  • For the plots overlooking the streets with a width of 30 meters or 36 meters, it is prohibited to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots to become completely commercial. It is allowed to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots so that they become residential only.
  • It is possible to benefit from the residential displayed plots by using car parks without buildings, while maintaining the privacy of the neighbors and constructing fences on the side of the residential streets.

Communications Equipment and Supplies Sales Halls (Mobile Phone Kiosks) Requirements

Design requirements:


  • The area of the hall must not be less than 100 square meters, and the area of the hall must be proportional to the number of kiosks and the services supporting it.
  • The area of the kiosks must not be less than 8 m2 with dimensions of 2x4 m.
  • Each kiosk should have a sliding door for the possibility of closing when needed.
  • Such doors must not open to the direction of the street.
  • An office must be provided for the supervisor in the hall.
  • The height of the display tables must not exceed 1.2 m.
  • The width of the corridors at the opposing kiosks must be less than 2.4 m.
  • A minimum parking space must be provided for each kiosk.
  • Adequate toilets must be provided for workers and shoppers.
  • A buffet must not be allowed inside the hall.
  • Public safety requirements must be provided and coordinated with the Civil Defense.
  • Adequate lighting must be provided for the kiosks and corridors.
  • The front facade must be transparent.
  • The area of the kiosks must not exceed 60% of the hall area, and the rest will be corridors, public toilets and customer waiting areas.
  • Requirements of people with special needs must be taken into account with regard to corridors, toilets, parking lots, entrances and exits.

Site Requirements:


  • The site must be located on a commercial street or within a commercial complex.
  • The entry and exit must be from the commercial street.
  • The length of the plot on the commercial street must not be less than 20 m.
  • The hall must be on one floor only or in a ground floor.
  • Hall plans demonstrating the kiosks according to the requirements of the secretariat must be provided.
  • Licensing of a facility with statutory violations is prohibited until the violation is corrected.

  • For the plots overlooking the streets with a width of 30 meters or 36 meters, it is prohibited to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots to become completely commercial. It is allowed to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots so that they become residential only.
  • It is possible to benefit from the residential displayed plots by using car parks without buildings, while maintaining the privacy of the neighbors and constructing fences on the side of the residential streets

Residential Tower Buildings Requirements
  • Commitment to the building code with regard to setbacks, the number of floors and the construction ratio of the floors, entry and exit.
  • Setback of upper annexes from the border of the last floor facing the streets must not be less than 2 meters
  • Task of determining the space for apartments in residential towers (with high density) shall be left to the designer and the owner, with commitment to the parking spaces required for the residential unit and the requirements and elements of the residential apartment.
  • Design of the facades in a modern and aesthetic way must be taken into account, with a commitment to providing privacy in the neighborhood of the residential area.

  • For the plots overlooking the streets with a width of 30 meters or 36 meters, it is prohibited to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots to become completely commercial. It is allowed to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots so that they become residential only.
  • It is possible to benefit from the residential displayed plots by using car parks without buildings, while maintaining the privacy of the neighbors and constructing fences on the side of the residential streets
  • In the event that there is any commercial activity with an entrance separate from the entrance to the residential tower, the parking spaces are calculated according to the type of activity, in addition to the parking spaces of the tower.
  • Balconies, staircase and elevator area, upper annex area, and service floors are not calculated as part of the building parameters.
  • In the commercial Al-Asab area and the strip located west of King Fahd Road, floors of the strip located east of the Olaya General Street and the strip located east of the General Olaya must be doubled by making service floors within the building, provided that these floors are calculated as part of the overall floors number and the regular building parameters in accordance with the following regulations:
    1. The number of floors intended to be a service floor must not be less than eight (8) floors.
    2. The number of service floors must not exceed two floors in a building whose regular height starts from (9-16) floors, and only one floor for buildings whose regular height is 8 floors, i.e., a service floor for every eight floors.
    3. The height of the service floor must not be more than (3m).
    4. The service floors must be visible on the facade as service floors and are treated architecturally in a way that is different from the normal floors of the building.
    5. The coverage percentage of the service floor must not exceed the coverage percentage of the recurring floor allowed by the building code.

Hotel Buildings Requirements on a Land Area Greater than 10,000 m2
  • For the plots overlooking the streets with a width of 30 meters or 36 meters, it is prohibited to merge the commercial plots with the displayed residential plots to become completely commercial.
  • The land must be located on a main street, the width of which must not be less than 60 meters.
  • The minimum area of the plot of land must be 10,000 square meters, and the length of the plot must not be less than the width of the main street.
  • The maximum construction parameter must be 3.
  • The maximum coverage of the land with buildings must be 40%.
  • The maximum height of the hotel building must be 15 floors within a depth of 70 meters, and for any depth exceeding that, the height of the buildings must be two floors.
  • The minimum setback for the building from all sides must be 12 meters.
  • Parking spaces must be provided according to hotel regulations (one parking spot for each 5-stars room and one parking spot for each 4-stars two room).
  • The entry and exit must be from the main street side only and within the limits of the commercial depth, and fences must be constructed, surrounding the project's sides overlooking the secondary streets, and openings for entry and exit to the project land on the secondary streets must not be made, except within the limits of the commercial plot only.
  • Privacy of the neighbors must be taken into account by making original architectural treatments.
  • Compliance with the regulations issued by the General Authority for Tourism and Antiquities related to hotels.
  • An integrated traffic study based on the technical and procedural guides for the traffic requirements of new urban facilities must be submitted, and approved by the Royal Commission for the City of Riyadh in its second meeting for the year 1414 AH held 7/29/1414 AH.
  • Coordination with the Saudi Electricity Company to locate the electricity room or generation stations within the project land.
  • Coordination with the competent authorities to deliver public services to the project at the expense of the owner, and the owner shall bear any additional financial burdens as a result of any increase on the existing infrastructure.
  • Coordination with the General Authority of Civil Aviation and Riyadh Air Base to ensure that the building does not fall within the runway of the air base.
  • The application shall be reviewed and audited along with all attached documents by the Riyadh Municipality before issuing the building permit, in order to ensure that all the conditions necessary for the issuance of the building permit are met.
  • Exceptions are made for the Al-Asab area, King Fahd Road and Olaya Street, from Al-Washem Street in the south to Prince Salman bin Abdulaziz Road in the north
  • Submission of a legal undertaking, which provides that the building is used in the same licensed activity and that no other activity is used.

Closed Residential Complexes (Compounds) Requirements
  • The site to be developed is required to be a closed residential complex located within an approved housing plan, and in the event that the land is raw (unplanned) or planned street network, it shall be required that it must be within a land designated for residential use according to the Land Use Planning for the city of Riyadh until 1450 AH, with the commitment of the project owner to provide the regular planning percentage for public services.
  • The site of the complex must be surrounded by streets on all sides.
  • The minimum area of a closed residential complex must be 20000 m 2, and this requirement is specific to closed residential complexes intended for the purpose of investment and not for private family use.
  • The project owner shall be obligated to implement the infrastructure for the entire project land and to coordinate with the public services agencies to deliver services to his project and implement the services of the entire project buildings.
  • The project owner shall be obligated to submit all planning studies for the site and to submit a study of the urban design of the project elements before the initial approval of the project.
  • The project owner shall be obligated to provide an integrated traffic study to determine the traffic requirements of the residents and visitors of the complex, and to be approved by the General Administration of Studies and Designs in the Riyadh Municipality.
  • Achieve the security requirements of the project through coordination with the Emirate of Riyadh region after the initial approval of the project.
  • In the event that there are valleys or reefs that penetrate the site of the project, a study of the drainage of torrents must be submitted in coordination with the Riyadh Municipality, while adhering to the royal orders regarding construction in the valleys' interiors.
  • Submit a soil test from one of the specialized offices.
  • The project owner shall be obligated to obtain professional licenses for service activities related to the complex only
  • Take into account the requirements of security and safety, civil defense systems, and traffic safety.
  • The project owner shall be obligated to set up a management of the complex or a Property Owners Association in the event of selling housing units.
  • The per capita share of commercial services within the complex shall be (0.75 x the number of residents of the complex).

  • The main use of the residential must be complex only.
  • The height of the buildings must be according to the building code adopted in the region.
  • The upper limit of the height of the surrounding wall must be 3 m, taking into account the submission of the designs of the fence within the initial submissions of the project.
  • The upper limit of the construction parameter for residential units must be 1.2
  • Setback must be one fifth of the street width, with a minimum setback of 3 m, and a minimum setback of 6m for streets with a width of 30 m or more.
  • It is necessary to implement an external wooded pedestrian sidewalk as side setbacks surrounding the complex from all sides
  • It is required that the facades of the complex must be free of any electrical or mechanical extensions, air conditioning and communications equipment
  • It is possible for the owner to benefit from the building code developed on the commercial streets in the event that the site overlooks them, provided that the entrances to the residential units on those streets from within the complex

Routes and Mobility:

  • Make storage paths with a width of 3.5 m and a length of not less than 10 m at the main entry and exit area of the complex within the setback
  • Achieve the hierarchy of the street network within the complex, and that all units overlook 12 m wide streets, including pedestrian corridors, coordination works, and others.

  • Allocate public parking spaces for visitors to the complex, and allocate parking spaces for people with special needs

Requirements for Developed Hospital Systems on Streets of Width of 40m or More
  • The land must be located on a main street, the width of which must not be less than 40 meters, and the minimum depth of the land must be 30 meters.
  • The maximum depth of the ground must be 70 meters, and for any depth exceeding that, the existing building code must be applied in that area, according to what was stated in the letter of the Supreme Authority No. 20120801028 dated 13/09/1433 AH.
  • The minimum area of a plot of land must be 3000 square meters.
  • The maximum coverage of the land with buildings must be 40%.
  • The maximum height of the hospital buildings must 6 floors.
  • The maximum building parameter must be 2.4.
  • The minimum setback of the building from the street must be 10 m, and the setback from secondary streets must be one fifth of the width of the street, with a minimum setback of 4m
